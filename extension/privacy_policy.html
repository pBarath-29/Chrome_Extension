Privacy Policy

ToS Clarity Chrome Extension  ·  Last updated: May 2026

ToS Clarity does not collect, store, or sell your personal data. No account is needed to use it.

What actually happens when you use it

When you click "Review Agreement", the extension reads the visible text on the page you're looking at and sends it (along with the page URL) to a backend server running locally on your own computer. That server then forwards the text to the Groq API, which runs an AI model to produce the analysis you see.

That's the whole flow. Nothing is saved anywhere once the analysis is done.

What leaves your computer

The only thing that leaves your machine is the document text and the URL of the page, which go to Groq's servers for processing. Groq handles this under their own Privacy Policy and Terms of Use. If that concerns you, you should read those before using this extension.

What we never do

  • We don't record or store your browsing history
  • We don't keep copies of documents you analyze
  • We don't use cookies, analytics, or any tracking
  • We don't require an account or any personal information
  • We don't send data anywhere other than your local backend and Groq

The local backend

The backend server runs on your own machine at 127.0.0.1:8000 by default. It only holds data in memory for as long as a single request takes, then it's gone. Nothing is written to disk.

Questions
