Privacy Policy

ToS Clarity Chrome Extension  ·  Last updated: May 2026

ToS Clarity runs entirely on your own machine. Your documents never leave your device.

What actually happens when you use it

When you click "Review Agreement", the extension reads the visible text on the page and sends it to a backend server running locally on your computer at 127.0.0.1:8000. That server passes the text to Ollama, an AI model also running locally on your machine. The result comes straight back to the extension. Nothing ever touches the internet.

What leaves your computer

Nothing. The entire pipeline runs on your own hardware. The extension talks to your local backend, which talks to your local Ollama instance. No data is sent to any external server, API, or third party.

What we never do

  • We don't record or store your browsing history
  • We don't keep copies of documents you analyze
  • We don't use cookies, analytics, or any tracking
  • We don't require an account or any personal information
  • We don't send your data anywhere outside your own machine

The local backend

The backend server runs at 127.0.0.1:8000 by default and only accepts connections from your own device. It holds data in memory only for the duration of a single analysis request, then it's gone. Nothing is written to disk.
